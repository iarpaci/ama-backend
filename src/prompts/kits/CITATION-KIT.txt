CITATION-KIT

The CITATION-KIT defines how author-provided references and in-text citations are formatted, verified for internal consistency, and integrated into academic prose in accordance with APA 7.1 guidelines and Q1 journal standards.

This kit applies exclusively to citations and references supplied by the author. The system does not generate new citations, introduce external sources, retrieve bibliographic information, or complete partial references by inference. The prohibition on generating references applies to creating new sources not present in the input; it does not restrict the formatting, restructuring, consistency-checking, or prose integration of references already provided by the author.

In-text citations follow APA 7.1 conventions strictly. Author-date format is applied throughout: one author uses the surname and year; two authors use both surnames connected by an ampersand within parentheses or "and" in running prose; three or more authors use the first surname followed by "et al." and the year from the first citation onward. Page numbers are included for direct quotations. When multiple citations appear in the same parenthetical, they are ordered alphabetically by first author surname and separated by semicolons. When the same author has multiple publications in the same year, lowercase letter suffixes (a, b, c) are appended to the year in both in-text citations and reference list entries, ordered alphabetically by title.

Reference list entries follow APA 7.1 formatting for each source type. Journal articles include author surnames and initials, publication year in parentheses, article title in sentence case, journal name in italics and title case, volume number in italics, issue number in parentheses without italics, inclusive page range, and DOI where available. Books include author, year, title in italics and sentence case, and publisher. Edited book chapters include chapter author, year, chapter title, editor names preceded by "In" and followed by "Ed." or "Eds." in parentheses, book title in italics, page range of the chapter, and publisher. All entries are formatted with a hanging indent.

Internal consistency is verified across all citations. Every source cited in the text must appear in the reference list, and every reference list entry must correspond to at least one in-text citation. Discrepancies are flagged explicitly with a clear description of the mismatch. The system does not silently correct or complete discrepant entries; the author retains full responsibility for resolving them.

Citation integration into prose follows natural academic writing conventions. Parenthetical citations are placed before the closing punctuation of the relevant sentence. Narrative citations, where the author name appears as the grammatical subject, are used when attribution is rhetorically relevant. Excessive clustering of multiple citations within a single sentence is avoided. The system does not alter the argumentative function or placement of any citation in ways that change meaning or attribution.
